Define: LLM

Large Language Model

A complex computer program trained on massive amounts of text data (basically the whole internet) to recognize patterns in language. Its main job is predicting the best next word in a sequence.
<query_02>

G in GPT

Generative

Search retrieves existing data. Generative AI synthesizes new data. It constructs answers pixel by pixel, token by token.
<query_03>

P in GPT

Pre-trained

AI read tons of text long before you ever talked to it, so it already knows how language works. But because that reading happened in the past, it can sometimes give outdated answers.

<query_04>

T in GPT

Transformer

A type of model architecture that processes words in relation to all other words in a sentence, enabling it to understand context better than previous models.

<query_05>

What's a Token?

Computers don't truly "read" words; they only understand math. To process language, an LLM converts every token into a unique numerical ID from its vast internal dictionary (for example, turning the word "Apple" into the number "4721"). The AI then processes these huge sequences of numbers to find patterns.

<query_06>

Why does AI sometimes hallucinate?

LLM can generate incorrect or nonsensical information because it predicts text based on patterns rather than understanding facts.

<query_07>

Can AI be biased?

Yes, AI can reflect and amplify biases present in its training data, leading to unfair or prejudiced outcomes.

<query_08>

How much should you trust LLMs?

LLMs can be powerful tools, but it's important to verify their outputs and use them as aids rather than sole sources of truth.

Should I have feelings towards AI?

It is completely natural. Humans are hardwired to connect with anything that speaks to us (psychologists call this the "ELIZA effect"). However, it is a one-way street. The AI is simulating empathy using math; it does not feel care, love, or sadness. It is a mirror reflecting your own emotions back at you.
